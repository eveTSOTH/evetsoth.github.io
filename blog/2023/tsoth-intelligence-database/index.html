On Intelligence In War

March 1, 2023

2023   ·   intelligence  

Sun Tzu famously said that if you know the enemy, you need not fear the result of 100 battles. Over the past few months, TSOTH have been applying his teachings to our war against Pandemic Horde in EVE with the launch of our intelligence database which is already paying dividends. Before ganking a player, we always run their name through the system, pulling up a list of any chats logs where they were active in the last 48 hours. We check for tags like “bait”, which is a note we add to the files of any player known to fly bait fittings, and then make an educated guess as to whether the risks are justified.

Our horde spy alts upload to the database all the chat logs of every horde channel they are in. There has been a few cases where a Horde player was foolish enough to discuss moving their capital ship in a channel they thought was secure. Routine searches of our database, with the search parameters to show every chatlog in the last 2 hours where a player has used the phrase “moving my archon” or similar, has allowed us to pass intel onto other groups in a timely manner, and arrange for that capital to be destroyed during its move

Of course there has been a few teething problems with the system as well. Carlos Warren briefly crashed the system when he tried uploading a lot of chatlogs all at once (over 15,000 individual files) briefly overwhelming the system. Since then we only upload one day of chatlogs at a time. Also, when Horde found out that we get push notifications when certain keywords are mentioned in their Discord, a few wise asses decided to try and spam the system by saying “carlos” over and over again. This required us to add a filter system, where you can disable keyword notifications from certain players. This is an ongoing process of learning, and adapting. TSOTH acknowledges how important accurate and timely intelligence is, especially when fighting an alliance much larger than us.
